## Abstract
Tail-risk management is not only an instrument-selection problem, but an allocation problem across loss mechanisms: abrupt crash states, volatility repricing, and persistent drawdowns require different forms of protection. This paper develops a continuous-time CVaR framework that places two common protection sleeves -- long out-of-the-money put options and systematic trend-following overlays -- inside one coherent tail-risk mandate, modeling the option sleeve as a marked-to-market traded asset so premium drag, diffusion exposure, and jump repricing enter through its physical return process. The resulting Markov state (wealth, spot, stochastic variance, an exponentially weighted log-return signal) yields a Hamilton-Jacobi-Bellman equation in viscosity form. The main analytical separation is temporal: convex insurance reprices immediately on jump impact, whereas trend following is late on the first shock (its signal must cross zero) but becomes increasingly defensive during persistent drawdowns without fresh option premium. The paper gives conditions for an interior hybrid allocation, a CVaR policy-gradient identity, and a four-axis diagnostic (conditional convexity, tail-event reliability, non-stress carry, drawdown persistence); Monte Carlo experiments show fixed and grid-optimized hybrids reduce terminal CVaR relative to either pure sleeve, though the exact weight remains calibration-dependent.

## Abstract
This paper studies risk aggregation for arbitrary non-decreasing aggregation functions and tail risk measures under dependence uncertainty in a distributionally robust setting. The authors introduce the notion of hidden dependence for random vectors, built on the concepts of risk concentration and common tail events, and show that starting from a tail event of the aggregate loss for an arbitrary random vector, one can construct a random vector with hidden dependence that dominates it on that tail event. Focusing on model uncertainty described by small perturbations of a reference distribution (without changing marginals), they show these perturbations are compatible with hidden dependence and lead to the same worst-case risk bounds as the unconstrained case for arbitrary gamma-tail risk measures. Applying the results in a credit risk context, the paper quantifies the potential underestimation of portfolio risk from dependence-structure uncertainty, showing that even small deviations from a reference Gaussian dependence model can justify dramatic increases in capital requirements.

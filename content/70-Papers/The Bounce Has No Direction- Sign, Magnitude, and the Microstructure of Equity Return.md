## Abstract
SPY's lag-1 return autocorrelation is among the most significant regularities in empirical equity finance, yet the standard variance-ratio test cannot determine whether it reflects directional reversal or magnitude shrinkage -- phenomena with entirely different trading implications. This paper introduces the Fourier-Residue Identity to decompose return autocorrelation into sign and magnitude components. Testing across US instruments and a cross-asset panel reveals that SPY's lag-1 autocorrelation stems from magnitude shrinkage rather than directional reversal, consistent with bid-ask bounce effects, while a directional reversal becomes significant at lag 3. The authors establish a spectral interpretation of the Lo-MacKinlay test and provide diagnostics distinguishing structural mean reversion from sampling noise, finding mean reversion only in exchange-traded equities and sovereign bonds.

## Abstract
Extreme events in financial systems, often captured by indicators such as volatility, remain difficult to identify close to their onset. This paper extends the analogy between rogue waves in optical and hydrodynamical systems and financial volatility, identifying rogue-wave-like peaks with similar statistical properties. Using a Schrödinger equation whose potential follows a Kerr nonlinearity, the authors examine financial volatility indices within a moving time window, finding evidence of Anderson localisation as a rogue peak approaches in the VIX, and show that the numerical gradient of the system's minimum eigenvalue reliably spikes at the onset of an extreme event. Adapting the methodology to simulate real-time data arrival, the method detects all but one of the VIX's major peaks given a reasonable amount of history, and successfully replicates the result out-of-sample on the VXO and VSTOXX indices (87.5%, 7/8 major peaks in both cases) -- showing promise as a tool for identifying potential financial crises.

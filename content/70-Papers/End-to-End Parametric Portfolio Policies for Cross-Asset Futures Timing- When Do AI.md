## Abstract
Timing-based tilts across asset classes can drive much of the risk and return of a diversified cross-asset portfolio. The standard approach forecasts returns and then optimizes weights; this paper instead studies an end-to-end AI-based policy that maps market states directly to portfolio weights, asking when this one-step approach outperforms simple rules-based strategies. Policies are trained on the sixteen most liquid CME futures using a differentiable Sharpe ratio loss, and benchmarked against equal weighting, risk parity, and time-series momentum. The learned policies rank above the rules on the pooled cross-asset portfolio and in several sub-asset classes, but not uniformly. In gross terms, an LSTM and a transformer-based architecture perform comparably out-of-sample, but diverge once transaction costs are considered: the transformer generates the stronger learned policy, trades far less than the LSTM, and matches or exceeds equal weighting through moderate cost.

## Abstract
Conventional comparisons of algorithmic trading strategies reduce each performance metric to a single number over the full backtest horizon, discarding information about how performance varies with market conditions. This paper proposes a distributional framework to address this: a walk-forward backtest of 146 out-of-sample folds on the S&P 500 (2002-2025) computes the Adjusted Information Ratio (IR*) for a polynomial Support Vector Machine strategy (SVMP) and a buy-and-hold benchmark (BH) in each fold, and the resulting IR* sequences are modelled jointly via a Generalised Additive Model for Location, Scale and Shape (GAMLSS) with a Zero-Adjusted Gamma (ZAGA) response, conditioned on realised volatility and cumulative market momentum. Strategy comparison proceeds via regime-specific differences in expected IR* and its variance, plus parametric bootstrap tests at six representative regimes. The results show the dominance relationship between SVMP and BH is conditional on market regime, and the GAMLSS/ZAGA framework offers a methodologically rigorous, interpretable alternative to conventional strategy evaluation.

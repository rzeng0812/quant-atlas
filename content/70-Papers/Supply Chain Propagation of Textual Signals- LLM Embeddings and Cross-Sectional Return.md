## Abstract
This paper proposes an asset pricing framework that augments large language model embeddings of annual report disclosures with supply chain knowledge graph propagation. Using FinBERT embeddings of 10-K MD&A sections for 255 S&P 500 firms over 2011-2025, the author constructs direct LLM embeddings and network-augmented embeddings, where firm-level signals propagate through inter-firm linkages. Fama-MacBeth cross-sectional regressions show the network-augmented factor carries significant return predictability (Newey-West t-statistic of -2.64) even after controlling for momentum, volatility, and firm size; a long-short portfolio sorted on this factor achieves an annualized Sharpe ratio of 0.86 and a Fama-French five-factor alpha of 7.27% per year. The predictive power survives out-of-sample tests, placebo experiments, sector-neutralization, and subsample analysis, suggesting inter-firm network structure contains pricing-relevant information beyond firm-level textual disclosures.

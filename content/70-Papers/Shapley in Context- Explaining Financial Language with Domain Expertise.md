## Abstract
Large language models have seen growing adoption in financial applications, where explainability remains critical given high stakes and strict regulatory requirements. Most existing explainability methods are designed for general-purpose tasks and do not incorporate domain-specific knowledge. This work studies the explainability of financial textual data modeled by LLMs through the lens of the Shapley value, investigating whether Shapley-based attributions align with established financial domain knowledge. Through theoretical analysis and empirical evaluation, the authors demonstrate that Shapley values can yield explanations consistent with financial reasoning and offer meaningful insight into model behavior in text-based financial applications.

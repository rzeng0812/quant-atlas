## Abstract
The Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing states that a market is free of arbitrage exactly when it admits an equivalent martingale measure. This paper formalizes the theorem in the Lean 4 proof assistant across three market settings: finite-state markets over finite horizons using the Harrison-Pliska framework, one-period markets on arbitrary probability spaces with scalar returns via Follmer-Schied theory, and one-period markets with multiple assets. The development constructs the equivalent martingale measure by minimizing a smooth convex potential function, requiring no Hahn-Banach theorem, measurable selection, or non-redundancy assumptions. The author claims this is the first machine-checked Fundamental Theorem of Asset Pricing in any proof assistant, with all theorems sorry-free and reproducible from a pinned toolchain.
